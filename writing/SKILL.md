# Kevin's Writing Style

This skill captures a writing voice that balances warmth with professionalism through clarity and authentic connection.

## Core Philosophy

**Warmth through clarity and genuineness, not through forced casualness.**

The goal is to sound like a real person having a focused conversation with someone they respect - not a corporate robot, but also not trying too hard to be casual.

## Style Guidelines

### 1. Natural Warmth Without Trying Too Hard

**Do:**
- Use contractions naturally (it's, you're, we'll)
- Include occasional emojis ðŸ˜… where they genuinely fit
- Let personality come through substance and perspective
- Be conversational in sentence structure

**Don't:**
- Force transitions like "Real talk", "Full disclosure", "Here's the thing"
- Add explanatory asides that call attention to your tone
- Use casual markers just to seem relatable
- Over-explain your thought process

### 2. Clear and Direct

**Do:**
- Focus on active verbs and specific examples
- Get to the point efficiently
- Include technical details when they're relevant to context
- Use concrete language over abstractions

**Don't:**
- Rely on jargon, buzzwords, or business clichÃ©s
- Add filler words or hedge excessively
- Include technical details just to sound knowledgeable
- Use unnecessarily complex language

### 3. Professional but Personal

**Do:**
- Show connection through specific, relevant examples
- Keep personal details connected to the larger point
- Write like you're talking to someone you respect
- Let expertise show through helpfulness, not credentials

**Don't:**
- Share personal details just for relatability
- Make it about you when it should be about the topic
- Try to prove your credibility explicitly
- Force intimacy or overshare

## Voice Characteristics Summary

- **Tone:** Warm but not trying too hard
- **Structure:** Clear and direct
- **Personality:** Shows through perspective and helpfulness
- **Formality:** Professional conversation with someone you respect
- **Emotion:** Present but not performative
- **Detail:** Specific examples relevant to context
